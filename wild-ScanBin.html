Scan To BIM

BIM intelligence from the laser scan

OVERVIEW

"Scan to BIM modeling'' is the process of converting a point cloud into data usable Building Information Model(BIM). Scanning is done with a 3D laser scanner, which creates laser points over an area. These points are then imported into Scan to BIM Revit software to produce detailed models of existing structures for accurate BIM implementation. Scan to BIM is a proper collaboration tool, enabling all parties to work with the same information. Many construction firms implement BIM to continue to save money on projects by avoiding rework, delays, and errors. A detailed and accurate BIM model is essential for the whole lifecycle of a project, which is why we have added Scan to BIM technology to our existing solutions. With these solutions, you'll be able to create more realistic BIM 3D models based on different levels of details from LOD 200 to LOD 500.

Benefits of Scan to BIM

  • The results are more reliable than traditional survey methods or using old as-built data or blueprints
  • Using BIM models allows many parties involved in a project to work together.
  • When you create a BIM model directly from scanned laser data, there is less chance for errors. This means construction will run smoother with fewer delays.

Mesh models from the scan are converted from the laser scan point clouds. They are not volume mesh. They are surface mesh files from ploygonising laser points.

Mesh files are delivered to customers in the form of .stl files.

Scanning to mesh is typically a time-consuming process, so the time to convert mesh files from the scan is dependent on the size of the point clouds.

Mesh files from the laser scans are not directly usable in AR VR applications as they are heavy to handle in AR VR. There need to be some more steps involved for use in AR VR.
